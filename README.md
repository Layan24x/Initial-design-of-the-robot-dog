# Initial-design-of-the-robot-dog
Simple Robotic Dog - Mechanical Design

Project Overview

This project presents the first mechanical design for a simple, four-legged robotic dog. The project aims to understand the required mechanical details, identify the basic components of the vehicle, and perform basic movements. To minimize the impact on young participants, microphones selected from those with advanced technical skills are used.

Objectives

* Design a simple frame for the robotic dog.

* Create four mechanical legs.

* Determine the number of joints and degrees of freedom.

* Select suitable servo motors.

* As required for the joints.

* Apply weight.

* Establish a basic gait pattern.

* Determine the mechanical conditions.

Mechanical Design

Structure

* Frame structure.

* Additional frame space for electronics and battery.

* Four attachment points for the legs.

Legs

Each leg consists of:

* Detailed drawing
* Knee joint
* Foot

This design allows for easy walking while maintaining stability.

⸻

Return of Freedom (DOF)

For each leg:

* Hip joint ← 1 degree of freedom
* Freedom joint ← 1 degree of freedom
* Knee joint ← 1 degree of freedom

Contact:

* 3 degrees of freedom x 4 legs = 12 centers of freedom

This mechanical design is simplified to meet project requirements.

⸻

Conveyor

Servo Motor:

* MG90S metal geared servo motor

* Torque: Approximately 2.2 kg·cm

* Operating voltage: 4.8–6 V

Controlls one servo motor per joint.

⸻

Specific Torque

Approximate Robot Weight:

* 1.2 kg

Approximate Range on One Leg:

* 0.3 kg

Using:

Torque = Force × Distance

Force = Force × Gravity Skill

Approximate Target Height:

≈ 1.05 kg·cm

Therefore, the MG90S motor provides sufficient control with safety features.

⸻

Random

The robot's core function is:

* The center of gravity remains temporarily off the body.

* At least three legs remain in contact with the ground while walking.

* Weight distribution is evenly distributed across all four legs.

⸻

Walking Method

A simple crawling method is suggested:

1. Remove the right side.

2. Permitted license papers.

3. Withdraw the license permit.

4. Remove the right rear side.

Conclusion

This concept embodies the basic principles of quadrupedal robots. Despite its simplicity, it covers essential topics including mechanical structure, the robot's gantry, actuators, torque estimation, balance, and cooling strategy. This design forms a solid foundation for future development into a more efficient robotic platform.
